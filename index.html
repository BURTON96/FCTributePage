The Life of Reni Eddo-Lodge

Reni Eddo-Lodge is an award-winning author and British journalist. In June 2017, Eddo-Lodge completed her critically acclaimed debut book, Why I’m No Longer Talking to White People About Race. Initial reviews were positive, with 2015 Booker Prize-winner Marlon James writing that it was “essential” and “begging to be written”

In March 2018, Eddo-Lodge’s podcast, About Race with Reni Eddo-lodge,, premiered. It was chosen as one of the best podcasts of 2018 by Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Harper’s Bazaar UK, The Guardian, British GQ and Wired UK .

In 2018, Eddo-Lodge won Bold Moves Award from Women in the Creative Industries for the publication of Why I’m No Longer Talking to White People About Race. In January 2018, British Vogue highlighted was‘new suffragette’. Prior to the publication of the book, in 2015, Eddo-Lodge won a 30 to watch award from MHP Communications. In 2014, the Guardian listed her as one of the 30 most exciting people under 30 in digital media. In addition to all this, she was also been listed in Elle Magazine’s 100 Inspirational Women list and The Root’s 30 black viral voices under 30.
